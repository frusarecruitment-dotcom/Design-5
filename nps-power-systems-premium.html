Yes — Florida requires permits for standby generator installations. We handle the entire permitting and inspection process for you, so your system is fully code-compliant and you never have to navigate the paperwork.

Cost depends on the size of your home, the load you want to back up, and your fuel source. As a general guide, residential standby units typically range from a few thousand dollars for the equipment plus installation. The right size depends on what you want to power — from essential circuits to your whole home or facility. We perform an engineered load calculation rather than guessing, so you're never oversold a unit that's too large or stuck with one that's too small.

Most residential installations are completed in one to two days on-site once permits are secured and equipment arrives. We coordinate the full timeline — site prep, fuel and electrical connection, transfer switch, and commissioning — and keep you informed at every step.

Most home standby generators run on natural gas or liquid propane (LP). We'll help you choose the best fuel source for your property and connect it as part of the installation.

We recommend professional maintenance at least once a year, plus oil and filter service based on run hours. Regular maintenance is the single biggest factor in whether a generator actually starts when an outage hits — which is why we offer flexible preventive maintenance programs.

Auto-start failures are usually caused by a weak battery, fuel supply issues, a tripped controller, or skipped maintenance. It's one of the most common — and most preventable — problems we see. We'll diagnose the root cause and get your system back to standby-ready.

Absolutely. We offer flexible preventive maintenance programs for both residential and commercial generators, with predictable annual cost and priority service — so your system stays reliable year-round.

Yes — a properly sized whole-home standby system can power your entire house, including air conditioning, as long as it's correctly matched to your load. We make sure your setup supports major appliances without overloading.

With a steady fuel supply, standby generators can run for days. For natural gas units the supply is effectively continuous; for propane, runtime depends on tank size. We'll help you plan fuel capacity for extended outages.

Modern air-cooled and liquid-cooled units are engineered to run quietly — far quieter than portable generators. We also consider placement to minimize noise for you and your neighbors.

In a hurricane-prone region like Tampa Bay, a professionally installed whole-home generator is a strong selling point and can add to your home's appeal and value. It signals reliability and storm readiness to future buyers.

Generators are installed outside on a level pad, a code-compliant distance from windows, doors, and your home. During the site assessment we identify the ideal location for performance, code compliance, and flood and storm resilience.

Yes. In flood-prone Gulf Coast areas we can elevate units on platforms or pads to keep them above expected water levels — an important consideration we factor into every coastal installation.

Load bank testing verifies your generator can carry its full rated load — not just idle. It's especially important for commercial and standby systems, where you can't afford to discover a weakness during a real outage.
